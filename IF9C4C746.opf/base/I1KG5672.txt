Foreword
by His Holiness the Seventeenth Karmapa, Ogyen Trinley Dorje
This ceremony of offering to the gurus was composed for many reasons by
Ogyen Trinley Dorje, called “Karmapa.” I read and carefully studied the
words and meaning of ceremonies composed by gurus of many traditions,
including the Pangkong Chagyapa Sutra, which was the earliest dharma to
reach Tibet; the Bodhicharyavatara by the Victors' Son Shantideva; the
Gurupuja by the mahasiddha Lingrepa; the Gurupuja by Panchen Lozang
Chögyen; the Gurupuja of All the Dakpo Kagyu by the guru Jamgön Lodrö
Thayey; the Shakyamuni Ceremony by the great scholar Mipam Jamyang
Namgyal; the Ceremony of the Elders by Shamar Chödrak Yeshe; and sutra
rituals composed by several great virtuous friends of the Sakya tradition. I
then carefully extracted from them all that accorded with our present needs.
I have designed this to be accessible to ordinary people. It was composed at
the Vajravidya Institute in the Deer Park at Varanasi, where the supreme
Teacher, Shakyamuni, taught the dharmachakra of the Four Truths to his
first five disciples.

A CEREMONY OF OFFERING TO THE GURUS
COMPOSED BY THE GLORIOUS GYALWANG KARMAPA, OGYEN TRINLEY DORJE

I salute the glorious Lion of the Shakyas.
From the Collection of Precious Qualities Sutra:
Buddhadharmas depend on virtuous friends.
This was said by the Victor, who possesses all supreme qualities.
In the words of the Kadampa Lords:
The first of all instructions
Is to never abandon the holy friend.
Faith, bodhichitta, and all qualities will arise,
Because he is their treasury.
Dakpo Rinpoche said:
If your guru's blessings do not enter you, you will never see this mind-in-itself by looking at
it. You will never grasp it by holding it. You will never stop it by suppressing it. You will never
abandon these self-appearances 110
 by rejecting them . You will never destroy them by demolishing

them . Therefore for those who want to gather the accumulations, purify obscurations, pacify
obstacles, generate realization, overcome appearances, and perfectly benefit themselves and
others there is nothing to do other than to serve, venerate, and supplicate the guru.
Mokchokpa said:
If Great Compassion is white, he's white. If Vajrayogini is red, she's red. If Hevajra is blue,
he's blue. Nevertheless, wherever I am , I'm never separate from the appearance of my guru.
It seems that Buddhahood in one lifetime comes from that.
As stated above, the guru, the virtuous friend, is the ground of all excellence in this and future
lives, the root of all paths, the best means of progress, the guide to higher rebirth and libera
tion, and the source of all instruction. If, knowing this, you give rise to genuine devotion, you
will see the meaning of dharmata.
From the Uttaratantra:
Self-arisen absolute truth
Is realized through faith.
Engage therefore in the practice of gurupuja, the root of all virtue and excellence. Its method:
Bless the ground, hall, and offerings.
Invite the field of accumulation and welcome it.
Offer ablution, drying, raiment, and salves.
Salute, offer, praise; and confess wrongdoing.

Gurupuja by the mahasiddha Lingrepa; the Gurupuja by Panchen Lozang Chögyen; the
Gurupuja of All the Dakpo Kagyu by the guru Jamgön Lodrö Thayey; the Shakyamuni
Ceremony by the great scholar Mipam Jamyang Namgyal; the Ceremony of the Elders by
Shamar Chödrak Yeshe; and sutra rituals composed by several great virtuous friends of the
Sakya tradition. I then carefully extracted from them all that accorded with our present needs.
I have designed this to be accessible to ordinary people. It was composed at the Vajravidya
Institute in the Deer Park at Varanasi, where the supreme Teacher, Shakyamuni, taught the
dharmachakra of the Four Truths to his first five disciples.
